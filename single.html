The new part 1

it's funny how we keep asking for new stuff but we miss the old. The new is very delightful but also very uncomfortable. The new sometimes requires of us what we don't have inside. The new requires that we give up some comfort, something we are used to, to some routine, some pleasures. And more often than not in the beginning it doesn't feel worth it at all. I picture Abraham leaving home, or Jacob having to run away. Or Paul used to working in 'Corporate Clergy' now having to live making tents and backpacking across the world preaching the gospel... The new might require you give up a Mansion for an Apartment or an Apartment for a Mansion both ways quite disconcerting. The new might require a Pickup instead of a Range Rover. So even as you go out into the new, the uncertain, the unplanned. God is in the new. Just like every new season, after the harvest we plough, and sow again...I pray for you as you go into the new crying and weeping carrying precious seed, you shall doubtlessly come back again rejoicing as you bring in your harvest.
